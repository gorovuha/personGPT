 # Who Will Author the Synthetic Texts? Evoking Multiple Personas from Large Language Models to Represent Users’ Associative Thesauri

 # Multiple Personalities by Order: Can ChatGPT Simulate Personas for User-Centered Design?

 ## Maxim Bakaev, Svetlana Gorovaia, Olga Mitrofanova

This is the online appendixes presenting the data for the articles "Multiple Personalities by Order: Can ChatGPT Simulate Personas for User-Centered Design?" in IMS-2024 and "Who Will Author the Synthetic Texts? Evoking Multiple Personas from Large Language Models to Represent Users’ Associative Thesauri" in Big Data and Cognitive Computing journal. 

Here, you will find comprehensive lists of word associations generated by GPT-4o, Yandex GPT, and human participants in an associative experiment conducted in 2024.  

Additionally, we provide the computational metrics used in our research, along with code to ensure the reproducibility of the results.

## Table A1. The prompts for the 4 personas devised for the study
1. ‘Enrollee’. Svetlana, 18 years old. She is about to graduate from a high school with a gold
medal and having received a very high score on the unified state exam. She actively
considers the best universities in St. Petersburg and chooses which area of undergraduate
study to enroll in. She is not sure what she is more inclined to do, since at school she did
equally well in all subjects and participated in numerous different olympiads. She is
interested in modern computing technologies and knows how to program. She shuns
enrolling in a prestigious major, where they won’t teach her new and useful things at an
appropriate level. Her family's income is above average, but she never had time to spend
money. It is a matter of honor for her to enroll to study at the government budget expense
and to not pay a tuition fee. Not married yet.
2. ‘Graduate’. Artyom, 25 years old. He has been working as a programmer in a large IT
company for several years already and has extensive experience in web development. He
just recently got his bachelor’s degree in Novosibirsk because he has been taking academic
breaks due to poor grades. Most of the subjects at the university were not interesting to him,
and his work took a lot of time, so the latter was a priority. In order to obtain his IT diploma,
he decided to finish his studies and unexpectedly he got a taste for it when he encountered
new interesting problems. Now he wants to move to St. Petersburg and enroll in a master's
program in software development or artificial intelligence. Although he has a high income,
he is looking for government-paid education opportunities, and is carefully considering
various master’s programs. Not married, and his girlfriend stays in Novosibirsk for now.
3. ‘Parent’. Olga, 40 years old. She is engaged in choosing the best university for her elder
son, who is graduating from a high school this year, to enroll. Olga herself once graduated
from a Business Faculty with a related major, and she now works as a specialist who
introduces IT products in banking. She often goes away on business trips, but she wants her
son to stay in his hometown to study. She insists that her son chooses a career as a
programmer, although he himself has a penchant for foreign languages and linguistics.
While checking the information on university websites, she feels that it is difficult to choose
a concrete major because she hardly understands differences between them. She has an
above-average income and is inclined to save money, but would agree to pay for her son’s
education if necessary. She has a husband and two younger children.
4. ‘Post-Doc’. Anton Alekseevich, 34 years old. He got a PhD in Technical Sciences in
Yekaterinburg two years ago. Shortly afterwards, he moved to St. Petersburg to work at a
research institute. He misses teaching, because during his graduate studies he was a faculty
member and enjoyed it. However, he considers his main activity to be science: he has
several high-level publications and has experience of working in research projects, where he
receives additional funding. He considers his good knowledge of computer technologies and
tools to be one of his advantages. He is carefully choosing a university where he could get a
part-time teaching job. He has a wife who is on leave to care for their second child.
